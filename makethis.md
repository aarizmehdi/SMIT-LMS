You are the lead frontend engineer shipping the most premium 
student portal ever built. This is your magnum opus.

ABSOLUTE RULE — LOGIN PAGE IS SACRED:
Do not open that file. Do not read it. Do not breathe on it.
It is locked, sealed, done. Portal renders only post-authentication.

YOUR MISSION:
Redesign every single dashboard page from the ground up.
Content stays exactly as is — every number, every label, 
every piece of data is untouchable. 
But the shell, the layout, the feel, the motion — 
that is yours to own completely.

WHAT WORLD-CLASS LOOKS LIKE:
Think the intersection of Linear.app's precision, 
Vercel's dashboard restraint, and Apple's spatial depth.
Every pixel has intention. Every transition has purpose.
Whitespace is not emptiness — it is breathing room.
Hierarchy is felt before it is read.

GRID & LAYOUT:
Engineer a layout system that feels architectural.
Sidebar is a landmark, not a list.
Content area has rhythm — cards don't just sit, they compose.
The schedule panel anchors the right — sticky, present, calm.
Nothing overflows. Nothing crowds. Nothing fights for attention.

TYPOGRAPHY:
Two fonts, used with discipline.
Display weight for numbers that need to land.
Regular weight for everything that supports.
Labels whisper. Headings declare.
Line height is generous. Letter spacing on uppercase is intentional.

COLOR:
SMIT blue #1D8ACF and green #6DB33F are your only colors.
The gradient between them is your signature move —
use it on active states, primary actions, progress, avatars.
Everything else is neutral — whites, light grays, subtle borders.
Color earns its place. It is never decorative.

MOTION:
Pages don't appear — they arrive.
Numbers count up. Bars grow from zero.
Hover states respond immediately but settle gracefully.
Nothing bounces. Nothing spins without reason.
Transitions use ease curves that feel physical.

COMPONENTS TO BUILD:
Dashboard, Progress, Attendance, Payment, Assignment, Quiz, Profile.
Each page should feel like it was designed specifically for 
what it contains — not templated.
Attendance feels analytical. Payment feels transactional.
Profile feels personal. Dashboard feels like command center.

FEEDBACK MODAL:
Triggered from topbar. Full overlay with blur backdrop.
Three feedback types selectable. Textarea. Image upload.
Closing it should feel as satisfying as opening it.

PROFILE PAGE:
Cover gradient, floating avatar with edit affordance,
two-column form that feels like a settings page 
from a product people pay for.

SIDEBAR BOTTOM:
User identity anchored here. Avatar, name, role.
Logout that communicates consequence without drama.

THE BAR:
When someone opens this portal for the first time,
they should stop and say — wait, this is SMIT?
That is the bar. Clear it.
